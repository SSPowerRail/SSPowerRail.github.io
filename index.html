Professional Profile

Technical Authority.
Commercial Awareness.

I provide independent technical consultancy, advisory support, and engineering oversight to clients delivering major railway electrification, signalling power, traction power, high-voltage infrastructure, grid connection, and renewables projects across the UK, Europe, the Middle East, Asia, and North America.

My career has been defined by accountability — holding senior engineering roles at Network Rail, Mace, Bechtel, Transport for London, WSP (Parsons Brinckerhoff), Amey, and Babcock Rail — consistently responsible for the technical integrity of complex, multi-disciplinary capital programmes. I have overseen projects from initial feasibility and option selection through to construction, testing, commissioning, and programme closeout.

My expertise spans AC-only, DC-only, and mixed AC/DC electrification systems in rail, as well as high-voltage power infrastructure and grid connection advisory for solar PV, battery energy storage (BESS), wind generation, and data centre supply projects from 11kV to 132kV.

As a registered European Engineer (Eur Ing) through FEANI, I am recognised across all EU member states — removing cross-border registration barriers and enabling direct engagement on transnational electrification programmes, European high-speed rail schemes, and continental infrastructure investment projects.

My value to clients lies in challenge, oversight, and independent technical authority — not design production. I work alongside your existing design and construction teams to elevate technical quality, reduce engineering risk, and provide the independent verification that major infrastructure projects demand.

Equally at home managing contractors, sitting on client-side technical review boards, conducting GRIP and PACE stage gate reviews, participating in equivalent project governance processes used across European, Middle Eastern, Asian, and North American rail and non-rail sectors, or providing on-call specialist advisory, I adapt my engagement model to suit the specific needs of your programme.

Engagement Model

Four Ways
To Engage

Engagements are structured to integrate smoothly alongside your existing delivery teams — providing independent technical depth, specialist challenge, and governance at the level your programme demands.

01
Independent Review & Verification
Acting as client-side technical authority or independent checker — providing structured review, challenge, and formal approval of contractor design submissions, with fully auditable written outputs forming part of the permanent project record. Suitable for framework or programme-wide appointment across rail, grid, and renewables sectors.
02
Advisory Retainer
Monthly retainer providing on-call specialist knowledge — answering complex technical queries, attending key design and progress meetings, reviewing emerging issues, and providing written technical guidance. Particularly effective for clients without in-house E&P or HV specialism, or for international programmes requiring UK-standard expertise.
03
Peer Review & Expert Opinion
Fixed-scope peer review of technical submissions, protection studies, tender returns, or design packages at project gate points — including GRIP, PACE, and equivalent international review stages. Provides independent expert opinion for due diligence, dispute resolution, value engineering, or bid evaluation.
04
Embedded Programme Support
Fixed-term or long-term embedded engagement within a client or programme team — providing full discipline lead capability, responsible engineer management, contractor challenge, and end-to-end technical governance. Equivalent to a senior in-house Principal Engineer role, without the overhead. Available UK and internationally.
